You want to secure your data to retain over the long term. What is the best solution?

(A) Virtualization

(B) ** Offsite backup

(C) Onsite clustering

(D) RAID 5 onsite backup

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It’s possible to utilize any of these choices for external hard drives, but I highly advise using one of them over the other. A solid-state drive (SSD) for an external backup will be an excellent choice if you plan on keeping the data for an extended length of time.

Concept note-2: -One of the best backup strategies to have is the 3-2-1 rule. This means creating 3 different copies of your data to put on 2 different storage types and keeping 1 copy offsite. By doing so, you’ll be able to create a failsafe system that keeps your data safe.

Concept note-3: -Full backup essentially makes a backup of everything you wish to protect every time. So, all files, objects, bytes however you wish to measure your data – every one of them is copied over to a secondary storage target each time. If you perform a full backup once a day – then everything is copied over once a day.

Concept note-4: -Digital Optical Drives. The maximum capacity of a modern DVD disc is 4.5 gigabytes, and 35 GB for Blu-ray, which is adequate for many small businesses. Conventional RAID Array. Off-Site Cloud Storage. External Hard Drives. Flash Drives.
