When deciding which cloud service provider to use, you should check:

(A) ** their disaster recovery plan

(B) ** backup policies and procedures

(C) ** cyber security policy

(D) ** are their systems compatible with yours

(E) what free gifts you would receive

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When evaluating whether a particular application is suitable for a cloud, some of the factors to consider are redundancy, the application’s ability to migrate, performance, security and cost: What level of system and network redundancy is needed to ensure 7x24 hour application availability, and at what cost?

Concept note-2: -Cloud interoperability is the ability of a customer’s system to interact with a cloud service or the ability for one cloud service to interact with other cloud services by exchanging information according to a prescribed method to obtain predictable results.

Concept note-3: -Platform/mobile compatibility. Make sure the cloud application is fully functional on the multiple operating systems and Web browsers that are likely in use at your organization. Also verify its accessibility via mobile devices if needed. It’s important to think about the future when it comes to compatibility as well.
