Resource Provisioning means

(A) Selection of software and hardware resources

(B) Deployment of software and hardware resources

(C) Run-time management of software and hardware resources

(D) ** All of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Resource provisioning means the selection, deployment, and run-time management of software. (e.g., database management servers, load balancers)

Concept note-2: -Resource provisioning has to do with assuring that the cloud service will be sufficiently resourced as customer demand increases. To do this, a CSP would need to take certain measures to successfully deliver on its SLAs. For instance, the CSP might have procedures in place to add servers or storage as demand increases.

Concept note-3: -Cloud provisioning is the allocation of a cloud provider’s resources and services to a customer. Cloud provisioning is a key feature of the cloud computing model, relating to how a customer procures cloud services and resources from a cloud provider.

Concept note-4: -Water, food, wood and other goods are some of the material benefits people obtain from ecosystems called ´provisioning services´. Many provisioning services are traded in markets. However, in many regions, rural households also directly depend on provisioning services for their livelihoods.

Concept note-5: -System provisioning is the process of setting up infrastructure in the cloud, including establishing user, system, and service access to the applications, data, and cloud resources.
