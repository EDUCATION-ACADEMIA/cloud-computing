If you use gmail, your emails are probably stored locally.

(A) True

(B) ** False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Which of the following is an example of remote data storage? If you use gmail, your emails are probably stored locally. web applications-or web apps-that run in the cloud and do not need to be installed on your computer. You can store all types of information in the cloud, including files and email.

Concept note-2: -What makes cloud storage different? Instead of being stored directly on your own personal device (the hard drive on your laptop, for example, or your phone), cloud-based data is stored elsewhere-on servers owned by big companies, usually-and is made accessible to you via the internet.

Concept note-3: -Every Google Account comes with 15 GB of storage that’s shared across Google Drive, Gmail, and Google Photos.

Concept note-4: -Here’s the most basic definition of cloud vs. local storage: Cloud storage stores files on remote servers connected through the internet and owned by someone else. Local storage stores files on physical server equipment on your premises, either in flash memory or hard disk drives.
