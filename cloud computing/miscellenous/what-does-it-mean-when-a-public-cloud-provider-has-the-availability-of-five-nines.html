What does it mean when a public cloud provider has the availability of five nines?

(A) No downtime

(B) Unavailable for potentially five minutes a day

(C) Unavailable for potentially five minutes a month

(D) ** Unavailable for potentially five minutes a year

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The accepted availability standard for emergency response systems is 99.999% or “five nines” – or about five minutes and 15 seconds of downtime per year (see table below). To achieve five nines, all components of the system must work seamlessly together.

Concept note-2: -Availability is normally expressed in 9’s. For example, “5 nines uptime” means that a system is fully operational 99.999% of the time-an average of less than 6 minutes downtime per year. The chart shows what impact various availability levels have on your server downtime.

Concept note-3: -The term five 9s refers to system and network availability. It’s a metric that measures uptime-that is, how often your system is up and running. Typically it’s measured in percentage of availability per year, usually ranging from 99% to 99.999% of the time.

Concept note-4: -The five nines standard originated in the telecom industry as a part of an effort to minimize downtime. 99.999% availability indicates that an environment will have five minutes or less of downtime per year (see Table A). Achieving five nines availability involves removing all single points of failure from a system.

Concept note-5: -4 9s (99.99%) scenario. This availability goal for applications requires the application to be highly available and tolerant to component failures. The application must be able to absorb failures without needing to get additional resources.
